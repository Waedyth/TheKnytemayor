Command Basics - /give

A position is any location within a Minecraft world. They define the locations for all of the entities, blocks and game events. They can be one of 3 types. world, relative or local. Let's make one of our own, and for simplicity, we'll have it be a world position.


10 5 15


"Okay, that was very simple!" Exactly. That's what I was going for. However, I have not told you what the three numbers do. The first number is the x coordinate. This is how far along the x axis the subject is in blocks (I will refer to the measurement as bl on this site). The second number is the y coordinate. That's how far the subject is vertically, in blocks as well. The third number is the same as the last two, but in the z axis. You may have guessed it, it's called the z coordinate.


Now with this knowledge, let's come up with another position. This time, let's make it relative.


~-24765 ~63 ~157


Here, the offset is the same for each coordinate, but now the position is relative to the target. Let's try local now.


^3 ^2 ^5


local is quite different to the other two types of position. This one has 3 values much like the others, but their meaning is different. The first value is not how far away the subject is in the x axis, but despite it being called x rotation, it is the rotation from the 'eye' mark on the entity's hitbox (or directionality of the block, or north if the facing block state does not exist) towards the left (anti-clockwise if observed from above).
